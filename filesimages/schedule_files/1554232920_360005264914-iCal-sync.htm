Connect to a channel

If there is no application to connect BookingSync to the channel you like, you can still use iCal to sync your calendar FROM BookingSync TO the channel.

Note that this connection is only 1-way and not instant. The synchronisation can take up to 6 hours to happen. Tentative is not shown as blocked with iCal sync.

For the channels connected through iCal, we highly recommend not to use instant booking as the risk of double bookings would be very strong.

Each channel has it's own way of importing an external calendar. We've listed below a few. Please refer to your channel support to get this information.

Synchronize your calendar with your "Google Calendar"

Source https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/37118?hl=en

After you've exported your events, you can import them into Google Calendar.

  1. Open Google Calendar on a computer. Note: You can only import from a computer, not a phone or tablet.
  2. In the top right, click Settings.
  3. Open the Calendars tab.
  4. Click Import calendars between the "My calendars" and "Other Calendars" sections.
  5. Click Choose File and select the file you exported. The file should end in "ics" or "csv"
  6. Choose which calendar to add the imported events to. By default, events will be imported into your primary calendar.
  7. Click Import.

If you're using a ZIP file, find it on your computer and open it. You'll see ICS files for each of your calendars. Take the individual files out of the ZIP file, and import each ICS file individually.

Note: Repeating events might not show up that way if you import them from a CSV file, but they'll be on your calendar as a series of one-time events.

Synchronize your calendar with your iOS calendar apps (iPhone, iPad, iPod touch)

After exporting your BookingSync calendar in iCal, you can subscribe to it from your iOs calendar app to get the regular updates.

  1. On your device navigate to Settings > Calendars.
  2. Select Add Account
  3. Choose Other
  4. Go to Add Subscribed Calendar
  5. Paste the iCal link and go Next
  6. Save
Note that tentative is not shown as blocked with iCal sync.

Why we don't recommend 2 way synchronization with iCal

At BookingSync we make the software so that no double bookings can ever happen. That's why we don't do 2 way synchronization with iCal (particularly import iCal into BookingSync).

We technically can, but this would lead to potential double bookings. Here is a step-by-step example of the problem if we were to do so:

a) Let's say you receive a booking on some of the portals where you've listed your property on a channel which is not connected to BookingSync, apart from an iCal import. As the iCal import is not instant (nor reliable), the booking might be updated 1 hour after it has been confirmed on the channel.
b) In the meantime, you receive a confirmed booking from another source to book a similar date with instant booking (Airbnb, Booking.com, ..)
c) As the calendar is "still free", the second booking is created. and you are now with a double booking situation...

As you can see, this solution can make advertising portals "connected" in both ways, but none would be considered as your calendar of reference. So there would be no way to accept instant booking with it, but also you would need another system to handle your business reservations.

With BookingSync, double bookings should never happen. You just have to follow a certain process. Itâ€™s proven to work and to be reliable.

It is that simple:

  1. You receive a booking request
  2. You update one calendar (BookingSync calendar)
  3. You accept the booking on the portal
  4. BookingSync synchronizers do the rest â€“ updating the calendars on all BookingSync supported portals where you have listed your property


Please note, new reservations should be entered first in BookingSync before they can be confirmed on other sites.
